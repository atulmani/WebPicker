Other than the Italians it is us who take Italian food seriously!

Having been in the business of spreading the love for Italian food for almost three decades, the brand, Little Bird is synonymous with amazing food. It exudes the same home-like warmth and distinctiveness that is reflective of how the world perceives Italy. Our journey began in 2002, in Pune. when our founder Mr. Shaka Pandey realized the need for a Italian fine dining format in India. In the last two decades, Little Italy as a brand has blossomed beyond everyone’s expectations, with 20 outlets across 5 cities in India!.

Risotto

Risotto is a creamy Italian dish that tastes a lot like the rice version of mac and cheese.

The story of risotto began in the 14th century B.C., when the Arabs brought rice to Sicily and Spain during their rule. Italy was the ideal place to grow short-grain rice due to the humid weather and abundant flat land. As a result, rice became a main part of Italian food culture.

Penne fruit salad

Sweet lime and orange with spring vegetables like capsicum and carrots makes a lovely vitamin C rich salad. Best to boost your immunity with this Penne and Fruit Salad and keep infections at bay. Penne and bean sprouts impart a blend of soft and crunchy textures to the salad.

Pasta, of course, is associated with Italy where they take it very seriously. But pasta salad is actually more American than Italian. Italians generally prefer their pasta hot and served as a separate course.

Spaghetti Soup

Spaghetti Soup is so warm, comforting, and bursting with flavor. Convenient canned spaghetti sauce and beef broth.

Spaghetti comes from the word Spago, which translates in English to “string,” or “twine.” In Italy, spaghetti (like all pasta) are generally cooked just to al dente (which means “to the tooth.”) to create a slightly chewy texture, rather than an overly soft consistency.

Ambrosia Salad

Ambrosia Salad is a creamy fruit salad loaded with pineapple, mandarin oranges, coconut and miniature fruit-flavored marshmallows.

The name references the food of the Greek gods. It originated in the 19th century. In 2017, "ambrosia" was the most-searched-for Thanksgiving dish by people in Vermont.

Macedonia Di Frutta

Macedonia di Frutta is an Italian name for the best in the world alternative to fresh fruits.

Macedonia or macédoine is a salad composed of small pieces of fruit or vegetables. Fruit Macedonia is a fresh fruit salad and is a common dessert in Greece, Romania, Spain, France, Italy and Latin America.
